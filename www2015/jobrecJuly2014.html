We're Hiring: MEMS Engineer

Our successful and growing MEMS product development firm is looking for a versatile and experienced MEMS engineer who thrives on intellectual variety and challenges. Our company serves customers from small startups to Fortune 500 companies around the world. We design and build all types of novel MEMS for consumer, medical and industrial applications.

Job Description

This job requires an engineer who can manage working on multiple diverse projects simultaneously, with timelines varying from one month to several years. You will design and prototype a wide variety of custom MEMS devices, sometimes as project leader and sometimes as a team member. Typical project work consists of helping the customer develop a specification, developing initial concept designs, performing advanced MEMS design and modeling, process integration and mask layout, prototype fabrication (at UC Berkeley Marvell Nanolab, equipment training will be provided) and working with foundries to transfer technology for volume production.

For projects that you lead, you will be expected to manage client expectations and communications, and to keep your projects on time and on budget. In our business, excellent project management is as important as doing excellent engineering work. You may also perform internal R&D, write proposals, travel to meet customers/suppliers, and represent the company at professional events. You will be expected to continually develop your engineering expertise and knowledge of all things MEMS.

Requirements

  • Self-directed, organized, and detail-oriented with a can-do attitude, proven multi-tasker
  • Can tolerate ambiguity and a variable workload (which can range from 30-55 hours/week)
  • Excellent engineering fundamentals, with university degree(s) in Engineering or Physics
  • At least three years of industry work experience with design of piezoresistive or capacitive MEMS devices
  • Advanced mask design (L-edit preferred) and process integration skills
  • Project management experience
  • Hands-on MEMS fabrication experience
  • English fluency; excellent communication and interpersonal skills
  • U.S. Citizen or Permanent Resident (green card). F1-OPT visa will be considered only if the work experience requirement is met.

Desired Skills

  • 10 or more years of experience in MEMS (may include advanced degree)
  • UC Berkeley Nanolab or other fabrication facility experience
  • ANSYS, SoftMEMS, Matlab, and/or SolidWorks
  • MEMS chip packaging, sensor testing, circuit and/or ASIC design

Benefits

  • PPO medical insurance, dental and vision plans, 401k, paid time off

Compensation

  • Paid hourly, rate commensurate with experience
  • Variable workload ranging from 30-55 hours per week
